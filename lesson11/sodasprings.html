The Station

by Chris Fowler - 11/26/2020

James Fowler was a prominent citizen of Soda Springs, Idaho. Around 1950, him and his brother, Vaughn Fowler built a Beeline gas station on the east end of Soda Springs, highway 30. James owned and operated that service station until he sold it around 2008. During that time, it changed from Beeline to Husky to Sinclair. James Fowler was known to most people as Jim, but I knew him as grandpa.

Every summer I would spend a week at my grandma and grandpa Fowler's house. On some mornings in the summer, I spent time with Jim at his station. I learned to watch Jeopardy, do crossword puzzles, and I watched him do oil changes in the service bay. I remember how special I felt when he would let me pick a soda out of the cooler, or a candy off the shelf. Sometimes he would even let me reset the pumps. Jim had a daily excercise routine that he completed each morning at his station. All of his grandkids knew that Jim kept a pack of wintergreen gum in the desk drawer, along butterrum candy and cinnamon lifesavers in his pocket.

There are many things I remember from spending time with grandma and grandpa Fowler each summer. I remember going fishing with Jim, swimming at Lava Hot Springs, watching the red-hot slag of Monsanto ooze down the hillside, and drinking soda water from the naturally carbonated spring at Hooper Spring Park. One of the things I will always cherish is spending time with Jim at his Sinclair gas station.
